1Use Case

Choose the Right Size for Your Room

The size of your new TV highly depends on which room it will be used for.

Bedroom

32" - 43" TV

Ideal for personal viewing in a smaller space. A compact screen keeps things comfortable from your bed or dresser.

  • HD Ready
  • Full HD
  • Smart TV

Living Room

50" - 65" TV

The sweet spot for family viewing. A larger screen fills your living room without overwhelming the space.

  • 4K Ultra HD
  • Smart TV
  • Android TV

Entertainment Room

70" - 85" TV

Go big for a cinematic home theatre experience. Pair with a soundbar or surround system for full immersion.

  • 4K Ultra HD
  • OLED / QLED
  • 120Hz

Gaming Setup

43" - 65" TV

Gamers need fast refresh rates and low input lag. Prioritize TVs with Game Mode and HDMI 2.1 support.

  • 120Hz
  • HDMI 2.1
  • Game Mode

Kitchen / Dining

24" - 32" TV

A smaller TV works best in tight spaces. Great for catching the news or your favorite shows while you cook.

  • HD Ready
  • Full HD
  • Basic TV

Everyday Listening

32" - 43" TV

Use your TV as a monitor or for video calls. Full HD is sufficient; look for multiple HDMI and USB ports.

  • Full HD
  • Smart TV
  • HDMI

4Types

Know the Types

STANDARD LCD PANELS

LED / LCD

The most common panel type. Uses a backlight behind an LCD screen. Reliable, bright, and affordable.

Best for: Everyday use

QLED

Quantum dot technology enhances LED backlighting for brighter, more vibrant colors — especially in well-lit rooms.

Best for: Bright rooms

Mini LED

Thousands of tiny LEDs give finer backlight control, resulting in better contrast and fewer blooming issues vs standard LED.

Best for: HDR content

PREMIUM SELF-EMISSIVE PANELS

OLED

Each pixel produces its own light and can be completely turned off for perfect blacks and infinite contrast. Superior picture quality vs LCD.

Best for: Dark rooms, cinephiles

QD-OLED

Combines quantum dot color with OLED self-emissive pixels — delivering the best of both worlds: brighter OLEDs with ultra-vivid color.

Best for: Premium setups

HDR expands the range of colors and brightness levels a TV can display, making bright scenes pop and dark scenes more detailed.

6Specs

TV Specs, Explained Simply

What those numbers on the spec sheet actually mean — in plain terms.

01

Resolution (p or K)

The number of pixels on screen. More pixels = sharper, more detailed image. 720p is basic; 4K is four times the detail of 1080p.

Look for: 4K (3840x2160) for TVs 50" and above

02

Refresh Rate (Hz)

How many times per second the screen redraws the image. A higher Hz means smoother motion — critical for sports and gaming.

Look for: 60Hz minimum; 120Hz for gaming & sports

03

Contrast Ratio

The difference between the brightest white and the darkest black a TV can display. Higher contrast means deeper blacks and punchier highlights.

Look for: OLED (infinite contrast) or high native contrast ratio

04

Nits (Brightness)

Measures how bright the screen gets. More nits = better visibility in brighter rooms and more impactful HDR highlights.

Look for: 16-32Ω mobile · 64-250Ω desktop
