    5Features

    Features Worth Paying For

    You don't need every feature—just the ones that match your use.

    Noise Cancellation (ANC)

    Actively counters ambient noise in real time. Transforms offices and commutes into cleaner listening environments.

    Prioritize if: you work in noise or need to focus for hours.

    Battery Life

    Check the earbud charge time and the case's total capacity — that's the number that matters for travel.

    Look for: 6+ hrs per charge, 20+ hrs total with case.

    Microphone Quality

    A good mic picks up your voice, not the room. Essential for meetings, calls, and online classes.

    Must-check for: WFH, students, frequent callers.

    Comfort & Fit

    Specs don't matter if you can't wear them long. Look for adjustable bands and multiple ear tip sizes.

    Critical for: sessions longer than 4 hours.

    Device Compatibility

    Check Bluetooth version, 3.5mm jack support, and platform compatibility before buying.

    Always verify: especially for gaming console setups.

    Water Resistance (IPX)

    IPX4 handles sweat and splashes. IPX7 survives submersion. Moisture damage is rarely covered by warranty.

    Minimum: IP44 for gym, IPX6+ for outdoor sports.

    6Sound Basics

    Sound Specs, Explained Simply

    What those numbers on the spec sheet actually mean — in plain terms.

    01

    Driver Size

    Bigger drivers produce fuller bass — but tuning matters more than size. A well-tuned 10mm can outperform a poorly tuned 15mm.

    Look for: 40mm+ headphones · 10mm+ earphones

    02

    Frequency Response

    The range of sounds the device reproduces. Human hearing is 20Hz–20,000Hz. Wider range = more complete sound.

    Look for: 20Hz – 20,000Hz baseline

    03

    Sensitivity

    Measured in dB, this is how loud the output gets for a given amount of power. Higher sensitivity means more volume from low-power devices like phones.

    Look for: 20Hz – 20,000Hz baseline

    04

    Impedance

    Low impedance (16–32Ω) works with phones. High impedance (64Ω+) needs a dedicated amp to reach full volume and detail.

    Look for: 16–32Ω mobile · 64–250Ω desktop
